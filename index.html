Julian Assange – Cypherpunks

The book asserts that states fundamentally operate through the threat or application of violence. Property rights, taxation, censorship, and intellectual property laws are all enforced by state-sanctioned coercion.

Key Themes

  • The State as a System of Coercive Violence

    The book argues that the foundation of state power lies in its ability to enforce laws and regulations through coercion. This includes property rights, taxation, censorship, and intellectual property laws, which are maintained through the application or threat of violence.

  • Encryption as Non-Violent Resistance

    Cryptography is framed as a revolutionary tool to neutralize state violence. Unlike physical resistance, encryption cannot be overpowered by brute force, making it a uniquely democratic weapon.

  • Mass Surveillance and the "Info-Pocalypse"

    Highlights the rise of surveillance infrastructure post-9/11, justified by societal threats such as terrorism, child pornography, drug trafficking, and money laundering. These measures blur ethical boundaries and expand state control.

  • Corporate-State Collusion

    The book critiques the merging of corporate and state interests, where corporations facilitate surveillance and states legitimize monopolistic control over digital infrastructure.

  • Technological Centralization and Economic Efficiency

    Discusses how centralized systems, driven by economic efficiency, enable mass surveillance. Server farms and data centers create vulnerabilities that are exploited by state agencies.

  • Free Software and Decentralized Infrastructure

    Advocates for open-source software and decentralized networks, such as Tor, as essential tools for resisting corporate and state control over communication channels.

  • Knowledge Sharing as Cultural Evolution

    Defends unrestricted knowledge exchange as fundamental to human progress. Critiques copyright regimes and censorship as tools that stifle creativity and democratic participation.

  • The Future of Freedom

    Warns of a dystopian future dominated by global surveillance, contrasting it with the potential for a “high-tech rebel elite” using cryptography to preserve autonomy.

Significant Quotes

“First, recall that states are systems through which coercive force flows. Factions within a state may compete for support… but the underpinnings of states are the systematic application, and avoidance, of violence.”

“Cryptography is the ultimate form of non-violent direct action.”

“While nuclear weapons states can exert unlimited violence over even millions of individuals, strong cryptography means that a state… cannot violate the intent of individuals to keep secrets from them.”

“The Four Horsemen of the Info-pocalypse: child pornography, terrorism, money laundering, and The War on Some Drugs.”

“The blurring of the state and corporation… the NSA and Google have a partnership in cyber-security for US national defense reasons.”

“The internet could exist without this centralization… it’s just that it is simply more efficient to have it centralized. In economic competition, the centralized version wins out.”

“Free software is so important for a free society.”

“The history of the human race… is the history of copying thoughts, modifying and processing them further on.”

“We will end up in a global totalitarian surveillance society… the last free people will be those who understand how to use cryptography.”

Terence McKenna – Food of the Gods

Food of the Gods by Terence McKenna is a groundbreaking exploration of humanity's relationship with psychoactive plants and their role in shaping our evolution, culture, and consciousness. McKenna argues that hallucinogens like psilocybin mushrooms were instrumental in the development of human self-awareness, language, and spirituality. He critiques modern society's suppression of altered states of consciousness and calls for an "Archaic Revival"—a return to the shamanic practices that once connected us to nature and the transcendent.

Key Themes

  • Psychoactive Plants and Human Evolution

    McKenna posits that psychoactive plants catalyzed the emergence of human consciousness, creativity, and culture. These plants allowed early humans to explore altered states of consciousness, leading to the development of language, art, and spirituality.

  • The Fall into Dominator Culture

    The book traces humanity's shift from a partnership-based society to a dominator culture that seeks to control and exploit the natural world. This shift is linked to the suppression of shamanic practices.

  • The Archaic Revival

    Proposes a return to ancient wisdom traditions and psychedelic exploration as antidotes to modern alienation and ecological destruction.

  • Language as Psychedelic Phenomenon

    Suggests that the origin of human language may be connected to psychedelic experiences and altered states of consciousness.

Significant Quotes

"The mutation-inducing influence of diet on early humans and the effect of exotic metabolites on the evolution of their neurochemistry and culture is still unstudied territory."

"We in our anguish traded partnership for dominance, traded harmony with nature for rape of nature, traded poetry for the sophistry of science."

"The suppression of the natural human fascination with altered states of consciousness and the present perilous situation of all life on earth are intimately and casually connected."

"Psychedelics are illegal not because a loving government is concerned that you may jump out of a third story window. Psychedelics are illegal because they dissolve opinion structures and culturally laid down models of behaviour and information processing."

Themes and Ideas

Nature of State Power and Control

  • States are systems through which coercive force flows, applying violence or its threat to maintain authority.
  • Mass surveillance has become the tool for state control, driven by fears of internet-induced governance disruption.
  • The merging of state and corporate power, exemplified by partnerships like NSA and Google, intensifies control mechanisms.

The Role of Cryptography in Resistance

  • Cryptography is non-violent direct action, a defense against coercive state control.
  • Encryption is inherently stronger than decryption, empowering individuals against surveillance.
  • Privacy and security are available but come at a cost, necessitating a deeper understanding of cryptography.
